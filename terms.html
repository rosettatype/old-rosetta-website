Payment method

Rosetta accepts credit/debit card payments via payment service provider Stripe. Contact us via email (see below) if you wish to pay via bank transfer or if you wish to purchase our merchandise.

Pay with card

In the webshop section of the website, select font(s) you wish to purchase licence for and proceed to payment. Fill in your purchase details and confirm the payment. You have to agree to our End-user licence agreement (EULA) to proceed. When the payment is complete, you will receive an email with receipt/invoice and download link for the fonts within a day after your payment. The font files will be available for download for thirty (30) days after the purchase.

If you somehow lose the fonts and need to download them again, contact us via email and we will send you a new download link.

Custom orders

If you wish to make a custom order (e.g. higher amount of computer licences, specific usage), please do not hesitate to contact us and we shall provide you with an invoice payable online. We will deliver the fonts via email after payment.

Delivery

The fonts are delivered as a zip-archive. Should you have any problem with their installation do not hesitate to contact us.

Merchandise is shipped within seven (7) business days and delivered worldwide. Unless otherwise specified, all rates include shipping by regular mail.

Value Added Tax (VAT) policy

Non-EU customers are not charged Czech VAT (21 %). EU customers are charged their local VAT unless they provide a valid VAT ID during the checkout process, in which case they are charged 0% VAT. Furthermore, with proof of business registration, we can cancel the VAT on ‘services’ for any European business entity upon enquiry. However, EU customers who were charged 0% VAT from us are required to declare and pay the associated value-added tax (the so-called ‘reverse charge’ procedure) in their country.

This policy follows legislation defined in the Czech VAT law 235/2004, Article 9 of VAT Act No. 234/2004 Coll., and European Council Directive 2006/112/ES.
